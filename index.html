About Me

I am broadly interested in using machine learning to model, analyze, and understand complex scientific systems. My work spans data-driven modeling of physical processes, structural analysis of brain and neural networks, and studies of how architectural design influences generalization and efficiency. Across these domains, I focus on methods that integrate mathematical structure, domain knowledge, and empirical validation—whether through optimizing network modularity, analyzing temporal dynamics, or probing the behavior of overparameterized models. Uniting these directions is a single goal: to develop interpretable and principled learning systems that reveal how data reflects the underlying laws of nature, rather than merely fitting patterns within it.

Voltage-Based State of Health Prediction for Lithium-Ion Batteries Under eVTOL Duty Cycles Using Temporal Convolutional Networks

MD Naeem Hossain, Umme Hani Prova, Ridowana Tabassum
The 6th International Conference on Steel, Power and Construction Technology (ICASPCT 2025)

To ensure safe and reliable flight in eVTOL systems, I developed a lightweight deep learning model to track lithium-ion battery degradation over time. Using only partial voltage and temperature data, the model accurately predicted health across 22 batteries — without needing full discharge tests.
